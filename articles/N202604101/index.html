Two India-spec EVs in The Yawning Chihuahua's Rear Seatbelt Reminder Evaluation

10.04.2026



The rear seatbelt reminder (SBR) evaluations for two models have been published today: the Tata Punch.ev and Mahindra XUV 3XO EV.

Recently facelifted, the affordable electric Tata Punch.ev receives a FAIL result: its rear seats have no occupant detection. Even for a system without occupant detection, the Punch.ev presents a unique problem: while most seatbelt reminders on the market without occupant detection tend to wait for a belt to first change status (thereby failing to notify many unbelted occupants), the Punch.ev's rudimentary seatbelt reminder instead issues a second-level audio and visual signal when any rear seatbelt is not fastened, regardless of occupancy. These "ghost" alerts can annoy the driver to the extent of encouraging tampering with the system or perpetually keeping the rear seatbelts fastened.

On the contrary, the recently launched Mahindra XUV 3XO EV receives a PASS: it notifies the driver with a second-level audio and visual warning if and only if a seatbelt is not fastened on an occupied rear seat.

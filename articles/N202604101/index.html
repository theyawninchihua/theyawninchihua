Two India-spec EVs fail TYC's Rear Seatbelt Reminder Evaluation

10.04.2026



The rear seatbelt reminder (SBR) evaluations for two models have been published today: the facelifted Tata Punch.ev and Kia EV9.

Tata's affordable electric SUV, the Tata Punch.ev, receives a FAIL result: its rear seats have no occupant detection, and it issues its second-level audio and visual signal when any rear seatbelt is not fastened, regardless of occupancy.

Even more surprisingly, the expensive, imported Kia EV9 sold in India does not behave like some of its overseas versions do, and receives a FAIL result: it would fail to notify unbelted occupants in common scenarios. Based on its documentation, the India-spec version's rear seat belt reminder does not have occupant detection, and only issues an audio signal when a rear seatbelt is fastened and then unfastened on the move. This is unlike the behaviour seen in EV9s destined for Europe and Australasia, which have occupant detection in the rear outboard seats, a prerequisite to score seat belt reminder points in those markets' respective consumer test programmes Euro NCAP and ANCAP.

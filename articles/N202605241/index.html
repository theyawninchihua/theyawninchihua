Maruti Suzuki WAGON R and DZIRE rear seatbelt reminders evaluated in-person in The Yawning Chihuahua's latest Rear Seatbelt Reminder Evaluation

24.05.2026



The Maruti Suzuki DZIRE and Maruti Suzuki WAGON R are the latest car models to undergo TYC's rear seatbelt reminder (SBR) evaluation, receiving FAIL results today.

The latest iterations of the popular DZIRE and WAGON R have seatbelt reminders as standard in all rear seats. The DZIRE and WAGON R in their "Tour S" and "Tour H3" avatars are popular taxis, and The Yawning Chihuahua recently had the opportunity to evaluate their rear seatbelt reminders in-person.

Maruti Suzuki DZIRE and WAGON R FAIL results explained

The following videos contain film demonstrating live how the DZIRE and WAGON R's rear seatbelt reminder behaves in the four test scenarios looked at in TYC's 2026 rear seatbelt reminder (SBR) evaluation.





As evident on film, the DZIRE and WAGON R's rear seatbelt reminders both behave purely based on belt status, and not seat occupancy. In order to avoid annoying users with false alarms (unlike the previous generation of Dzire and earlier versions of the Wagon R), the latest DZIRE and WAGON R do not start an audio signal on empty seats.

However, since the rudimentary system is incapable of monitoring seat occupancy, this behaviour comes at the cost of failing to notify even unbelted occupants. Instead, the audio signal is only triggered when an already fastened belt is unfastened, regardless of occupancy.

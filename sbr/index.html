About the Rear Seatbelt Reminder Evaluations
The guiding principle behind the evaluation is that an effective rear seatbelt reminder should never activate when a seat is unoccupied, and must activate if an occupied seat's belt is not fastened. Neither of these is required by forthcoming legislation, which only requires that the reminder signals activate when a belt is fastened and then unfastened at least on occupied seats.

In the Seatbelt Reminder Evaluations I perform a best-effort desktop assessment to estimate how the rear seatbelt reminder would behave in 4 scenarios. After interpreting this information I award a PASS/FAIL result to the system. The necessary and sufficient conditions to be awarded a PASS are:

It is important to note that occupant detection sensors are necessary but not sufficient in order to be awarded a PASS. Also note the Seatbelt Reminder Evaluation is also not assessing the volume, duration, period or position of the seatbelt reminder.

Vehicle Selection

A vehicle will only be evaluated under the 2026 Rear Seatbelt Reminder Evaluatons if it meets at least one of the following criteria:
  • never assessed under the 2025 Gobar Grades from defunct project Gobar NCRAP
  • significantly updated since last evaluated here or in the 2025 Gobar Grades. Updates considered significant include, but are not limited to: facelifts, generation changes, and changes in seatbelt reminder behaviour in any variant
  • if it is discovered that an error was made when the vehicle was last evaluated here or in the 2025 Gobar Grades

Error Policy

I strive to present the most accurate information possible; however, this being a desktop assessment, errors are bound to happen from time to time. The author does not accept responsibility for any damages resulting from use of information on this page, including but not limited to loss of property or life.

To improve information quality and accountability, I am adopting a policy of only evaluating vehicles based on official material on the manufacturer's India website (documentation or communications). Unlike the 2025 Gobar Grades, foreign-market documentation, PR communications, real-world test drives, and inference based on reviews will no longer be accepted.
